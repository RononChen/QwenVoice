# Reference baseline — pre-optimization, length sweep (2026-05-30, `670d5c8`)

Supersedes the single-length baseline (`baseline-2026-05-30-06166f0.md`) by sweeping **short / medium /
long** prompts across all 3 modes × both variants. This is the fixed reference point for backend/MLX
optimization; compare future runs with the same fixed corpus + cells. Generated by
`scripts/summarize_generation_telemetry.py` from runtime telemetry (raw diagnostics not committed).

## Conditions

- **Machine / tier:** native Apple-Silicon **8 GB** Mac → `floor_8gb_mac` (not forced).
- **Build:** `670d5c8` (no Quality→Speed OOM fallback; engine stamps `notes.promptChars`).
- **Telemetry:** `QWENVOICE_DEBUG=1 QWENVOICE_SUPPRESS_WARMUP=1`.
- **Matrix:** 3 modes × 2 variants × 3 lengths. Per cell **3 warm per length** (short/medium/long);
  Custom/Design add **1 cold** (medium) for load timing; Voice Cloning is warm-only (warm-by-design).
  59 takes. **Quality ran genuine 8-bit** on the native floor tier (every Quality `modelID` is `-8bit`;
  no OOM, no fallback).
- **Fixed corpus (reuse verbatim):**
  - short (~35 ch): `The train left the station at dawn.`
  - medium (~110 ch): `The morning train slipped quietly out of the station, carrying a handful of sleepy travelers toward the coast.`
  - long (~344 ch): `…toward the coast. Outside the fogged windows, pale fields gave way to grey water, and the rhythm of the rails settled into a steady, hypnotic hum. By the time the sun finally broke through, most of the passengers had drifted into an unhurried silence.`
  - Custom = default speaker (Aiden); Design brief = `A warm, calm middle-aged male narrator with a clear, measured pace.`; Clone = saved voice `A_warm_elderly_woman`.

## Output-quality gate

- **audioQC (objective):** 35 pass / 13 warn / 11 fail. **`dropout` flags scale with prompt length** —
  short (no comma): 16 pass / 0 fail; medium (1 comma): mostly pass/warn; long (2 commas + sentence
  breaks): most fail. One `clicks` flag (clone/quality/short, 27 events).
- **Listening pass (human, mandatory gate): PASS.** The `dropout` silences are the natural
  punctuation/sentence pauses (no chunk gaps, healthy RMS), and the `clicks` take had **no audible
  glitches** vs a clean take — both are detector false-positives on this corpus, not audio defects.
- **Follow-ups (detector tuning, not regressions):** `makeAudioQCReport` over-flags on natural content —
  (1) `dropout` counts punctuation/sentence pauses (raise the interior-silence threshold and/or ignore
  silence at punctuation); (2) `clicks` slew-detector trips on benign waveform transients. Tune both in
  the optimization phase so the tripwire stops crying wolf.

## Headline observations (optimization targets)

- **RTF < 1 in every cell (0.46–0.82)** — generation is slower than realtime on the 8 GB tier; the
  primary speed target. RTF rises slightly with length (fixed overhead amortized over more audio).
- **decode-loop is the wall-time driver and scales ~linearly with length:** short ~2.7–5.4 s,
  medium ~7.7–12.6 s, long ~29–41 s. Clone is the slowest per length.
- **Cold TTFC = model load:** Quality cold ~3.3–3.6 s, Speed cold ~2.9–3.1 s (the heavier 8-bit load is
  largely overlapped); warm TTFC ~1 s, length-independent.
- **Memory — peak physFoot (Jetsam-relevant), both variants:**
  - **Speed: ~2.65 GB – 4.75 GB.** Min custom/short (2.65 GB) → max clone/long (4.75 GB).
  - **Quality: ~3.70 GB – 5.62 GB.** Min custom/short (3.70 GB) → max clone/long (5.62 GB).
  - Quality runs ~0.9–1.0 GB heavier than Speed at the same cell. Both fit 8 GB; no OOM.
  - **Growth with length is modest (~0.3–0.5 GB short→long)** — KV cache for more tokens. Mode matters
    more than length: Clone > Design > Custom (clone conditioning + design instruct add resident GPU).
- **`trims = 0` everywhere** — no memory-pressure trims fired on the native floor tier during these
  generations (peaks stayed under the pressure thresholds).
